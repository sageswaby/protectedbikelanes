Department of Transportation Benchmark Target Unmet Leaving Kensington Cyclists at Risk

Kensington’s cyclists suffer amid DoT infrastructure delays.

By Sage Swaby

December 20, 2024

Kensington’s residents are pushing for safety measures for cyclists, amid lags in the city’s plan to construct bike safety infrastructure.

The Department of Transportation constructed 31.9 miles of protected bike lanes across the city in 2023 — 20 miles short of the benchmark it set for itself in the New York City Streets Plan, a five-year plan to enhance street safety. The slow progress opens up Kensington’s cyclists to the risk of death and serious injury.

Kensington is part of Brooklyn Community District 12, a Priority Bicycle district, a community district that has seen high numbers of cyclists killed or seriously injured.

“I haven't heard of any process starting in Community District 12 yet, which would be Kensington itself,” said Liz Denys, campaign lead at Flatbush Streets for People, a volunteer group of residents who advocate for road safety. “It just really points to how behind we are on this.”
